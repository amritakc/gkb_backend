Mission Statement

Since our founding in 2009, Good Karma Bikes’ mission has been to provide a path for transition age youth (18-26), and low-income individuals to gain self-confidence, work readiness, and an entry to college using a bike shop as a medium, providing both reliable, inexpensive form of transportation, and a platform from which to offer job training.

Good Karma Bikes approaches a variety of community problems through a unique business enterprise, that continues to demonstrate that our service model is cost efficient and effective, as well as environmentally sound.

COMMUNITY PROGRAMS

1:1

For each bike sold, we provide one bike or service for free.

Good Karma Bikes rehabilitates bicycles destined for landfill and transforms them into reliable and clean transportation. These same bicycles set the foundation for our community programming include:

Transportation for Transformation

We reinvest the money we make in our full-service bike shop into our programs that support our community.

Work-4-Bike

Targeted toward low-income individuals who cannot afford a bicycle, this provide provides a free bicycle in exchange for six volunteer hours.

Sponsor-a-Bike

For $50, community members can sponsor a bicycle in our work-4-program.

Saturday Clinic

A free, weekly bicycle repair clinic led by a group of dedicated volunteers, in which unsafe bicycles are repaired for community members.

Park Tool School

A one-month certification course in which participants learn cycling and bicycle mechanics.

Good Karma Kids

Targeted toward at-risk youth, this program pairs youth with adult volunteers to learn cycling and bicycle mechanics, in addition to learning the value of philanthropy.
